cPanel hosting vs Hostnodes
— which one is actually built for today?

cPanel has dominated web hosting for over 20 years. It works — but it's cluttered, legacy, and built on shared infrastructure. Hostnodes is container-based, fully isolated, controlled via OpenPanel, and free to start. Here's the full picture.

The core difference
cPanel hosting

cPanel is a control panel that sits on top of shared server infrastructure. All users on the same server share the same pool of resources — same MySQL server, same PHP process manager, same filesystem. One site's spike in traffic affects everyone else.

Shared environment — one compromised site risks all others on the server
Cluttered interface with hundreds of icons and nested submenus
Licence fees that increase every year, always passed on to you
No free plan — you pay from the very first day
Hostnodes via OpenPanel

Hostnodes is container-based. Every user gets a fully isolated environment — their own MySQL, their own PHP, their own Redis. It runs on OpenPanel, which is free and open, with no licence overhead. Think of it as having your own private slice of a server without managing a VPS.

Container isolation — each user lives in their own environment, fully separated
OpenPanel — clean, modern, open-source panel with zero licence cost
Honest, transparent pricing — and a genuinely free plan to start
Google Cloud infrastructure — not commodity servers in a shared rack
Why OpenPanel

Free to control. Not free to compromise.

OpenPanel is an open-source hosting control panel built from the ground up for container-based isolation. Unlike cPanel, it carries no per-seat licence — which means we don't have to pass yearly price hikes on to you.

It's the panel that cPanel should have evolved into: clean UI, per-user isolation, modern stack support, and a separation between admin and user roles that actually makes sense for agencies and resellers.

No per-seat licence cost
OpenPanel is open-source. We pass zero cPanel licence fees to you — ever.
Admin and user separation
Agencies manage clients without giving root access. Each client sees only their own panel.
Container-native from the start
Built for isolation, not retrofitted onto shared architecture like cPanel's jail shell.
Modern UI, not legacy clutter
Every tool you need, none of the 200 icons you don't. Clean, fast, purposeful.

VPS servers vs Hostnodes
— the power without the complexity.

A VPS gives you raw power but leaves you to figure out everything else. Hostnodes is ready to run the moment you sign up — fully accessible like a VPS, but managed so you can focus on building, not babysitting servers.

The core difference
Self-managed VPS

A VPS is a blank server. You get root access and that's it. Setting up NGINX, PHP, MySQL, SSL, backups, firewalls, and security hardening is entirely your job. One misconfigured permission or missed security patch can take everything down.

Hours of setup before you can deploy your first site
OS updates, patches, and security are entirely your responsibility
No panel — everything is command line, no abstraction layer
Backups only happen if you configure and remember them yourself
No free plan — minimum monthly cost even for testing
Hostnodes

Hostnodes gives you the isolation and access of a VPS — with a full control panel, managed infrastructure, automatic SSL, and daily backups included. You still have SSH access, PHP control, and database management. You just don't have to set any of it up.

Live in minutes — SSL, databases, PHP all configured automatically
We manage OS, patches, and security — you focus on your sites
OpenPanel gives you full SSH, PHP, MySQL, Redis access — all in a clean UI
Automated daily backups included — restore in one click, no config needed
Free plan to start — try it without spending a penny
Who it's for

Hostnodes is the right call if…

You're a developer
You want SSH, PHP control, and Git deploys — without spending your weekend setting up a server stack.
You run an agency
You manage 10, 20, 50 client sites. You want isolation between clients and a panel you can white-label as your own.
You're not technical
You need reliable hosting with a clean interface and someone else handling the infrastructure. That's exactly what this is.

Privacy policy

Last updated: 11 April 2026

1. Who we are

Hostnodes Ltd operates the Hostnodes web hosting service. We are committed to protecting your personal data under the GDPR and UK GDPR. Our data centres are in Frankfurt, Germany and New York, USA.

2. What data we collect

We collect account information (name, email, hashed password); billing data processed via Stripe; usage data (server logs, IPs, browser type); support communications; and session cookies plus optional analytics cookies.

3. How we use your data

We use your data to provide the service, process payments, send transactional emails, respond to support, detect fraud, comply with legal obligations, and improve the platform. We never sell your data or use it for advertising.

4. Legal basis for processing

Our GDPR legal bases are: Contract (providing the service); Legitimate interests (security and fraud prevention); Legal obligation (record-keeping); and Consent (optional analytics cookies, withdrawable at any time).

5. Data storage and transfers

Hosting data is stored in the region you select. Frankfurt data stays within the EU/EEA. New York transfers are governed by Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs).

6. Data retention

Account data is kept while your account is active. Billing records are retained 7 years for tax compliance. Server logs are deleted after 90 days.

7. Your rights

You have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, object to, and port your personal data. Contact privacy@hostnodes.com. We respond within 30 days.

8. Cookies

Strictly necessary authentication cookies cannot be disabled. Optional first-party analytics cookies can be turned off in your account settings. No third-party advertising or tracking cookies are used.

9. Security

We use TLS in transit, AES-256 at rest for sensitive fields, container isolation, vulnerability scanning, and role-based staff access controls.

10. Changes

We will email you at least 14 days before material changes take effect.

11. Contact

Privacy: privacy@hostnodes.com. General: hello@hostnodes.com.
