Frequently asked questions.

What is an SSL certificate?
An SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) certificate is a small data file that cryptographically binds your domain to an organization’s identity. When installed on a server, it activates the padlock in browsers and the HTTPS protocol — encrypting every connection between your visitors and your server.
Why do I need an SSL certificate?
SSL is required for any site accepting payments, logins, or personal data. It’s also a Google ranking signal, and modern browsers flag non-HTTPS sites as “Not Secure” — costing you trust and conversions.
What is the difference between DV, OV and EV?
DV verifies you control the domain (quick, automated). OV adds organization vetting (1–3 days). EV performs the most rigorous organization checks (3–7 days) and shows your company name in the certificate details — ideal for ecommerce and banking.
What is a Wildcard SSL certificate?
A Wildcard certificate secures your main domain plus unlimited first-level subdomains under it (e.g. *.example.com covers api.example.com, app.example.com, and so on) — all on a single certificate.
How long does SSL issuance take?
DV certificates issue in minutes. OV takes 1–3 business days. EV takes 3–7 business days due to extensive vetting.
